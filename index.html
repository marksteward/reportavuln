You've found a nasty vulnerability and want to do the right thing.


Now what?

What

Doing the right thing basically means telling those responsible for the service or product that it is vulnerable and is putting its users at risk.

Non Disclosure

Let them know, then sit back, relax and forget about it.

The idea here is that you do not disclose the vulernability to the public. Instead, you notify the owner, provide them with the relevant details then let them get on with it. Unfortunately, a lot of vendors and services do not take responsiblity for such security problems and could simply ignore your notification without ever fixing the problem. This means users remain at risk.

Responsible Disclosure

Let them know, but give them a timeframe in which to fix the issue. Then go public.

Instead of just notifying them and forgetting about it, you can give them a time frame in which they need to fix the issue before you go public with the vulnerability. This puts pressure on the vendor to actually fix it, otherwise they could face bad publicity and further ramifications of the details going public.

The danger here is that they might still ignore you which means you either have to give in or go public. If you go public, you might be directly putting the users at risk as anyone can now use that vulnerability. Having said that, it isn't your responsibility and you did try. Maybe.

Coordinated disclosure

Full Disclosure

No warning. Go public.

Here the details are published in full without warning to the vendor or service. This immediately exposes users to risk but could result in a swift response by the vendor.

You'll probably want to go with reponsible disclosure.

Who
